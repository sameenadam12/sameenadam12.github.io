Renewable Energy Generation: AI to estimate best geographical locations

Project Overview:
This project focused on developing AI-driven predictive models to forecast energy consumption and generation across multiple renewable sources, including solar, wind, and hydro energy. By analyzing historical energy usage data, weather conditions, and demographic factors such as GDP and population, we trained various machine learning models to identify optimal geographic locations for renewable energy investments

Key Features:
  • Data Sources: Historical energy consumption data, weather conditions (temperature, humidity, wind speed), and socio-economic data (GDP, population, inflation)
  • AI Models: LSTM for time-series energy consumption prediction, DNN for weather-based power prediction, and XGBoost for wind and solar power generation.
  • Target Variables: Energy consumption, power generation from renewable sources, and optimal investment locations.
Methodology:

The project employed the LSTM model to capture the time-series patterns in power consumption, while the DNN model leveraged weather data to predict energy consumption based on factors such as temperature, humidity, and wind speed.

Results:

The LSTM model accurately predicted power consumption trends with minimal error, providing insights into future energy demands.
The DNN model performed well in predicting power consumption based on weather conditions, achieving a MAPE of 3.3%.
The XGBoost model effectively predicted wind and solar power generation based on weather data, highlighting key factors like wind velocity and ambient temperature.

Technologies Used:
  • Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib)
  • TensorFlow and Keras for deep learning models
  • XGBoost and DecisionTree for regression models

Injury Based Contract Optimization for Athletes

Project Overview:
This Power BI project optimizes football player's contracts based on injury predictions. By using AI models built in Python, clubs can reduce financial losses due to injuries by adjusting player wages based on injury history and risk, as well as incentivizing players to stay fit.

Key Features:
  • Used Scikit-Learn in Python to train injury prediction models.
  • Interactive dashboard visualizes injury risks, player wages, and savings potential.
  • Comprehensive analytics for injury history and current contracts for all players.
  • Allowed clubs to adjust player wages based on fitness, incentivizing players to stay fit.
  • Clustering of all players into 7 classes based on injury risk and current wage contracts.
  • Select any Premier League player to view injury risk, current contract, and recommended optimized contract.
Technologies Used:
  • Python (Scikit-Learn) for machine learning and predictive modeling.
